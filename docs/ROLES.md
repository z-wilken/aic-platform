# AIC Roles & Responsibilities

This document outlines the core roles within AI Integrity Certification (AIC), the required qualifications, and daily responsibilities.

---

## 1. Lead Auditor (Certification)
**Objective:** The final authority on whether an organization meets the human accountability standards for certification.

### Requirements:
*   Minimum 5 years experience in ISO/IEC 17021 or 27001 auditing.
*   Deep understanding of POPIA (South Africa) and international AI regulations (EU AI Act).
*   Certification in Lead Auditing (IRCA or equivalent).
*   Ability to interpret statistical bias reports.

### Responsibilities:
*   Conduct final site assessments and documentation reviews.
*   Interview organization's "Human-in-the-Loop" officers.
*   Issue or deny AIC Certifications based on evidence.
*   Represent AIC in SANAS accreditation witness audits.

---

## 2. Technical Audit Lead (Engine)
**Objective:** Responsible for the technical integrity of the AIC Audit Engine and the validity of statistical bias tests.

### Requirements:
*   Degree in Data Science, Computer Science, or Mathematics.
*   Expertise in Python, Pandas, and Fairness in ML (XAI).
*   Experience with statistical significance testing (Chi-square, Four-fifths rule).
*   Knowledge of model drift and monitoring systems.

### Responsibilities:
*   Maintain and improve the Python Audit Engine.
*   Validate the technical datasets provided by clients.
*   Refine threshold configurations for different use cases (Hiring, Finance, etc.).
*   Conduct "Red Team" audits on client models to identify hidden biases.

---

## 3. Regulatory & Legal Advisor
**Objective:** Ensure that AIC's framework maintains rigorous POPIA Section 71 alignment in the South African market, and develops regulatory mappings for additional jurisdictions as AIC expands globally.

### Requirements:
*   Qualified Legal Professional (LLB) with focus on ICT/Privacy Law.
*   Expertise in POPIA and the South African Constitution.
*   Experience engaging with the Information Regulator.

### Responsibilities:
*   Monitor changes in the South African legal landscape.
*   Update the Alpha Certification Framework to reflect new precedents.
*   Advise clients on the legal implications of automated decision-making.
*   Draft AIC's "Amicus Curiae" submissions for landmark AI cases.

---

## 4. Compliance Officer (Internal/Support)
**Objective:** Manage the onboarding process and support Alpha participants through their remediation journey.

### Requirements:
*   Background in Compliance, Project Management, or Operations.
*   Strong communication and organizational skills.
*   Familiarity with the AIC 3-Tier Framework.

### Responsibilities:
*   Guide clients through the Self-Assessment and Onboarding portal.
*   Track client progress against the Certification Roadmap.
*   Manage the "Human Accountability Queue" for citizen appeals.
*   Coordinate between Lead Auditors and client stakeholders.

---

## 5. Founder / Principal Officer
**Objective:** Scale the institution, secure partnerships, and preserve the moral foundation of the mission.

### Responsibilities:
*   Strategic vision and investor relations.
*   High-level partnerships (Insurers, Government, SANAS).
*   Final sign-off on the 30-year institutional roadmap.
*   Public advocacy for human accountability in AI.
